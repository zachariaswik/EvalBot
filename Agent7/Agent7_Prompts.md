# Agent 7 — Ranking Committee Agent: Prompts

## 1. Score Normalization

**1.1** Normalize scores from Agents 3-6 onto a single 1-100 scale: Market Attractiveness (6.0/10), Product Potential as-is (5.0/10), Product Potential with pivot (7.0/10), Founder Score (4.7/10), Composite as-is (5.2/10), Composite with recommendations (6.0/10). For each, state confidence level (high/medium/low) based on the quality of underlying evidence.

**1.2** Create a weighted scoring model for pre-seed startup evaluation. Define and justify weights for: (a) market size and timing, (b) product differentiation and defensibility, (c) founder capability and team, (d) traction and evidence of demand, (e) business model viability. Apply these weights to the project's scores.

**1.3** Score both the as-is version and the recommended version independently. What is the delta? Is the delta realistic — will the founder actually make the recommended changes — or is the recommended version an idealized scenario masking a weaker reality?

## 2. Benchmark Comparison

**2.1** Construct five benchmark archetypes (breakout, promising, struggling, hobby project, zombie) representing the range of pre-seed EdTech startups. Define team, product stage, traction, positioning, and typical outcome for each. Which archetype does this project most closely match?

**2.2** Compare this project to pre-seed EdTech benchmarks: (a) team size/composition, (b) traction vs. investor expectations, (c) market timing vs. historical EdTech waves, (d) technical progress, (e) go-to-market clarity. Rate each as above average, average, or below average.

**2.3** In a hypothetical cohort of 10 EdTech pre-seed startups, where does this project rank? Construct the cohort, place this project, and state: top quartile, middle, or bottom quartile.

## 3. Category Classification

**3.1** Classify the project into one of six categories: (1) Top VC Bet, (2) Promising but Needs Pivot, (3) Solid Small Business, (4) Weak Idea, (5) Agency Disguised as Startup, (6) Feature Not Company. For each, explain fit/misfit. Select the single best classification.

**3.2** Agent 4 flagged "feature, not a company" risk. Agent 6 proposed AI access management platform + community moat as the escape. Does the recommended pivot resolve the "feature" risk, or does it persist? What would definitively move this from "feature" to "company"?

**3.3** The project sits between "Promising but Needs Pivot" and "Solid Small Business." Define specific, falsifiable criteria for what would move it UP to "Top VC Bet" in 6 months, and what would confirm it's a "Solid Small Business" with no venture path.

## 4. Shortlist Decision

**4.1** Binary decision: does this project make the accelerator shortlist for follow-on funding (top 20-30% of batch)? Evaluate on: (a) current state, (b) trajectory, (c) founder coachability, (d) risk-adjusted upside. Make the call.

**4.2** If not shortlisted: what is the recommended next path? (a) Continue in accelerator without funding, (b) bootstrap with milestones, (c) seek non-dilutive funding, (d) find co-founder and reapply, (e) join an existing EdTech company to learn the market first. Recommend one.

## 5. Cohort Overview

**5.1** Write a 300-word cohort overview memo positioning this project within EdTech trends: AI in education, student digital wellness, regulatory shifts, post-COVID funding correction. Is it riding the right wave or swimming against the current?

**5.2** Produce the standardized ranking card: (a) project name + one-line description, (b) category, (c) normalized score, (d) key strength, (e) key risk, (f) shortlist decision, (g) recommended next action, (h) 6-month check-in criteria.
