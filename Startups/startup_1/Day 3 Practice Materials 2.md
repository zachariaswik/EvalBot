# Day 3: Practice Tasks for Your Project

## Task 1. Your Focus Sentence and Experiment

Hypothesis

Complete the following sentences:

1. Our product does ONE thing perfectly: it helps students to achieve sustained
focus on their studies or project by using our kiosk-mode browser designed to
remove distractions and harmful content.

2. If we give students a way to remove all possible distractions, they will sustain
focus on their task.

## Task 4. Sales Pitch

Based on everything you learned during Days 1-3, draft your 5-minute sales pitch
script, sticking to the following structure (remember: it differs from your investor pitch;
use this one for your next user conversations):

● Problem: Make users feel the pain (60 seconds)

Over the last decade, there’s been a significant increase in distracting and addictive
applications in technology; Games, Social Media, Reels and constant notifications. The
new currency of the 21st century is our attention, and the most affected, with dire
consequences, are the younger generation.

School students need to use computers and the internet for learning, but their attention
is easily hijacked by various addictive applications.

● Solution:

Our solution is a browser with a focus mode, which locks the student within a learning
session - making it impossible for them to navigate to distracting and addictive
websites or any other harmful content on the internet.

● Demo: Show the core feature live, if possible. If it’s not ready, walk through the
ideal version (3 minutes)

We have a demo, but the gist of it is: We have a normal web browser, with a “Study
Mode” button, which lets the user quickly create a focus session with the tabs
conducive to learning. When the user enters this focus session, a kiosk mode opens
up, where URL bar is disabled and web search is highly filtered.

Depending on how the focus session was created, the user will either be locked within
the session with a timer, or experience high friction in leaving the session.

● Social Proof: Share who else is interested, demonstrate early results - anything that

gives pitch credibility (30 seconds)

We have pitched this idea to parents, teachers and teenage children - showing interest
in using and purchasing this solution.

This version of your pitch is a conversation starter. Use it, then adjust based on what
you hear.
