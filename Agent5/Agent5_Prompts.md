# Agent 5 — Founder Fit Analyst: Prompts

## 1. Founder-Market Fit

**1.1** The founder is a software developer building for K-12 students (with parents/teachers as buyer). Does the founder have a personal connection to K-12 education — as a parent, teacher, school administrator, or education researcher? Or is this a technically-driven founder entering a market they find interesting but don't deeply inhabit? What does the evidence across all practice materials reveal about the founder's proximity to the problem?

**1.2** The founder cites "shortened attention span," "brain-rot," and "AI cheating" as driving problems. Does the founder's understanding come from first-hand experience (own children, own classroom) or from general awareness? Does the founder demonstrate deep empathy for the end user (student) and buyer (parent/teacher), or is the understanding more intellectual than experiential?

**1.3** The founder claims "a network where I can get specialist input from computer science, design, marketing, education, entrepreneurship and business." Is this a deep, activated network (people committed, contributing) or a broad, passive one (people the founder knows but hasn't mobilized)? What evidence shows the network is actually being used?

## 2. Domain Expertise

**2.1** What domain expertise does the founder demonstrate in K-12 education, child development, digital wellness, or child safety? Check for: understanding of school procurement, COPPA/FERPA/GDPR-K awareness, knowledge of device management in schools, awareness of student psychology around restriction and autonomy. What is present vs. missing?

**2.2** K-12 EdTech has specific challenges: 6-18 month procurement cycles, IT department gatekeeping, FERPA/COPPA compliance, district budget approvals, teacher change management. Does the founder show awareness of any of these in the practice materials? What is the risk of entering K-12 without this knowledge?

## 3. Execution Ability

**3.1** The founder is "a 1-man team" with "a full-time job" and calls time "a big limitation." The roadmap allocates 40h/2 weeks (Milestone 2) and 80h/month (Milestone 4). Can a solo founder with full-time employment build, launch, and scale a K-12 product? Is the time allocation realistic or aspirational? What is the sustainable weekly output?

**3.2** "Agentic coding" is cited as a key strength three times across the materials. How much of an execution multiplier is it really? Where does it help (feature development, code output) and where doesn't it help (sales, compliance, design, user research, community building)? Is the founder over-indexing on coding speed as the advantage?

**3.3** Review the execution milestones (Day 3) and campaign plan (Day 4). Are these plans specific enough to execute? For each milestone: is the success criterion measurable? Is the resource allocation realistic? Is the deadline achievable? Is there a plan if the milestone fails?

## 4. Technical Depth

**4.1** Assess the technical complexity the founder must tackle: OS-level process management, browser engine integration, content filtering (HTTPS/DNS/proxy level), cross-platform support (Windows + Mac), kiosk security (preventing bypass via Task Manager, Alt+Tab, process killing). Is this achievable for a single developer? What are the hardest technical challenges?

**4.2** The founder says they're "a software developer and early implementer of Agentic Coding." What type of developer? The product demands systems-level expertise (OS hooks, browser engine, security hardening). Does the founder demonstrate awareness of these technical challenges, or is the description abstract enough to suggest the complexity may be underestimated?

## 5. Distribution Ability

**5.1** Distribution plan: (a) social media (Reddit, TikTok, Discord study communities) as primary, (b) educator networks for B2B. Does the founder have a social media presence or content creation track record? Has the founder demonstrated ability to reach educators at scale? What evidence exists that these channels will work?

**5.2** The Day 4 campaign plan says: "I can start immediately, by getting to know the specific Social Media channel." This is a learning plan, not a distribution plan — the founder hasn't yet built presence in these communities. How long does it take to build enough credibility in Reddit/TikTok/Discord to drive 100+ waitlist signups in two weeks? Is this realistic for a first-time social media marketer working part-time?

**5.3** Three potential investors identified: Brighteye Ventures, Emerge Education, Sparkmind.vc. All outreach is via events, LinkedIn, or portfolio founder connections. Does the founder have direct access to any, or is every path cold/semi-warm? How long does "connect on LinkedIn" typically take to reach a funded pre-seed round in EdTech?

## 6. Clarity of Ambition

**6.1** What is the founder's ambition level? Is this (a) venture-scale ($100M+, multiple rounds, large team), (b) sustainable growth (profitable at small scale, bootstrapped), or (c) unclear? What evidence points to each? The One-Pager mentions "100 million devices" but the roadmap targets 50 users. The SWOT says "1-man team" but investor research targets VCs.

**6.2** Is the founder driven by mission (solving the digital wellness crisis for children) or opportunity (market gap, buildable product)? Both are valid but lead to different companies. Which pattern does the evidence suggest, and what does that imply for persistence through hardship?

**6.3** "NOT READY FOR INVESTORS" appears three times in Day 5. Is this healthy self-awareness or a form of avoidance? What would "ready" look like, and is the current plan getting there fast enough? Does the founder's definition of "ready" match what pre-seed EdTech investors actually require?

## 7. Founder Scorecard and Missing Capabilities

**7.1** Beyond the identified need for "a developer and designer," what capabilities are missing? Consider: school sales/procurement expertise, COPPA/FERPA compliance knowledge, social media content creation, EdTech investor relationships. Rank missing capabilities by urgency and impact.

**7.2** Compile a Founder Fit Scorecard rating six dimensions (1-10): (a) founder-market fit, (b) domain expertise, (c) execution ability, (d) technical depth, (e) distribution ability, (f) clarity of ambition. For each: score, evidence, biggest risk. Provide an overall Founder Score with narrative assessment.
