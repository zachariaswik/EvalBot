# Agent 6 — Recommendation/Pivot Agent: Prompts

## 1. Strength Assessment

**1.1** Synthesize scores from Agents 3-5: Market Attractiveness (6.0/10), Product Potential (5/10 as-is, 7/10 with changes), Founder Score (4.7/10). Does this combination represent a viable startup? What minimum threshold is needed across these dimensions for a pre-seed investment decision, and where does this startup fall?

**1.2** Across all three agents, what is the single strongest signal that this startup could work, and the single strongest signal that it won't? Cite specific evidence.

**1.3** If you could change ONE thing about this startup to maximally improve its chances — not three things, not a roadmap, one thing — what would it be?

## 2. If Strong — How to Sharpen

**2.1** Agents 3 and 4 converged on AI access management as the strongest differentiated angle. If the founder leans in, describe the sharpened product: core experience, first user interaction, "aha moment," and what the parent sees that makes them say "I need this."

**2.2** Design the first 30 days of a sharpened go-to-market: what does the founder build, who do they talk to, what do they post, what do they measure, and what decision do they make at day 30?

**2.3** Design a 14-day validation experiment — without writing any code — that confirms or refutes: "Do parents of middle-schoolers actually want a tool that manages their child's AI access and blocks distractions during homework time?"

## 3. If Weak — Pivot Options

**3.1** If the core concept proves unviable (parents don't pay, students bypass it, platforms ship alternatives), what are the three most promising pivots using the founder's existing skills, network, and market knowledge? For each: product description, target user, why it's better, and first validation step.

**3.2** Would "the study browser for homeschool families" (3.7-4M students, 8.5% CAGR, no procurement friction) be a stronger starting point than "all K-12 students"? Analyze the trade-offs of pivoting to this narrower wedge.

**3.3** Could the founder pivot to a B2B infrastructure play — licensing the underlying technology (kiosk engine, AI classifier, browsing analytics) to GoGuardian, Securly, or Bark instead of competing with them? Evaluate the "picks and shovels" pivot.

## 4. If Too Small — Expand the Wedge

**4.1** SOM is $500K-$1M ARR in 3 years (lifestyle, not venture-scale). Map the concentric circles of expansion from wedge to $10M+ ARR: each new customer segment, required product changes, and approximate revenue potential.

**4.2** If AI access management proves successful, could it become the platform — with the kiosk browser as just the first product? Map the expanded vision: API, browser extension, MDM plugin, school-wide policy engine.

**4.3** Could the founder build the "Common Sense Media of student focus tools" — a brand-first, product-second strategy where community trust and content ratings create more value than any single product? Evaluate this approach.

## 5. If Just a Wrapper — Find the Deeper Layer

**5.1** The product is not an AI wrapper, but is it a "browser wrapper" — a thin UX layer on existing content filtering tech (DNS filtering, HTTPS inspection, whitelist management)? Where is the deeper systemic layer that creates unique value?

**5.2** The founder says: "Current tools block too much or too little; mine finds the sweet spot." Is this a structural insight (misaligned incentives, wrong architecture, wrong customer focus) or a feature preference ("I'll do it better")? If structural, articulate it. If not, the product is a feature, not a company.

**5.3** Map the evolution: wrapper → tool → platform → ecosystem. At each stage: what capability is added, what data is generated, what stakeholder is served, what lock-in is created? Define the minimum viable platform.

## 6. Synthesis — The Next Step

**6.1** Write a single-page "Next Move" document containing: (a) one sentence — what the startup IS, (b) one sentence — who the first 100 customers are, (c) three bullets — what to do in the next 30 days, (d) one sentence — the decision gate at day 30, (e) three bullets — what NOT to do, (f) one sentence — what "ready for investors" looks like.

**6.2** Evaluate three options: (A) continue current plan, accelerated; (B) sharpen around AI access management + middle-school wedge; (C) pivot to brand/community-first approach. Score each on: probability of 200+ users in 6 months, probability of pre-seed funding in 12 months, alignment with founder strengths, and risk level. Recommend one.

**6.3** Write a candid 200-word message directly to the founder: where they stand, their biggest blind spot, and the single most important thing to do this week. Honest, kind, specific.
